Baggage Reimbursement Policy Summary

## Liability Limits
- For domestic flights, maximum liability for lost, damaged, or delayed baggage is $3,800 per passenger.
- For international flights, liability is governed by the Montreal Convention and is limited to 1,288 Special Drawing Rights (approximately $1,700 USD) per passenger.
- We are not liable for incidental, punitive, or special damages.

## Reporting Deadlines
- Damaged bags: Must be reported within 24 hours of receiving the bag.
- Delayed bags: Must be reported at the airport within 4 hours of arrival.
- A formal claim with all documentation must be submitted within 30 days.

## Exclusions (Items NOT covered for reimbursement)
- High-value and special items: Antiques, artwork, computers and electronic equipment, software, fragile items, jewelry, keys, medication, money, negotiable papers, securities.
- Perishables.
- Normal wear and tear: Minor scratches, scuffs, dents, cuts, and dirt.
- Protruding parts: Damage to wheels, straps, pockets, pull handles, or other attached items.
- Unsuitably packed items.
- Damage to internal contents without damage to the exterior of the bag.

## Reimbursement Guidelines for Delayed Baggage
- We will reimburse reasonable and necessary expenses incurred as a result of the delay.
- Receipts are required for all claimed expenses.
- Examples of reasonable items: Toiletries, a change of clothes (e.g., shirt, underwear, socks), basic essentials.
- Examples of unreasonable items: High-end clothing, multiple outfits, luxury goods, items on the exclusion list.
